# Ethical Guidelines

Comprehensive ethical framework for the responsible use of the KVP Simulator AO Value Enhancement system.

## 🎯 **Core Ethical Principles**

### **1. Academic Research Purpose**
This system is designed exclusively for:
- ✅ Academic research and educational purposes
- ✅ Simulation modeling and theoretical analysis
- ✅ Open-source intelligence methodology development
- ✅ Understanding organizational structures for research

### **2. Prohibited Uses**
This system must **never** be used for:
- ❌ Operational intelligence gathering for real-world targeting
- ❌ Personal surveillance or stalking of individuals
- ❌ Commercial exploitation of personal data
- ❌ Harassment, doxxing, or privacy violations
- ❌ Malicious profiling or discrimination
- ❌ Real-time intelligence collection for operational purposes

## 📋 **Data Collection Ethics**

### **Public Information Only**
- **Principle**: Only collect publicly available information
- **Sources**: Government websites, academic publications, public institutional profiles
- **Restrictions**: No private social media, no authentication bypass, no private databases

### **Respect for Academic Freedom**
- **Research Content**: Do not analyze or reproduce academic research content
- **Privacy Settings**: Respect privacy settings on academic profiles
- **Attribution**: Properly attribute all data sources
- **Academic Integrity**: Follow academic community standards

### **Rate Limiting and Courtesy**
- **robots.txt Compliance**: Always respect website crawling guidelines
- **Rate Limiting**: Implement delays between requests to avoid server overload
- **Terms of Service**: Comply with all academic database terms of service
- **No Circumvention**: Never bypass anti-scraping measures or access restrictions

## 🔒 **Privacy Protection Framework**

### **Personal Data Minimization**
```
Collect Only What's Necessary:
├── Professional titles and positions ✅
├── Institutional affiliations ✅  
├── Published academic credentials ✅
├── Public research areas ✅
└── Private personal details ❌
```

### **Sensitive Information Handling**
- **No Collection**: Never collect sensitive personal information (SSN, personal addresses, family details)
- **Education Only**: Limit to published educational credentials and professional history
- **Public Research**: Only include research that's publicly published
- **No Inference**: Avoid inferring private information from public data

### **Data Retention Policy**
- **Temporary Processing**: Data used only for immediate research processing
- **No Long-term Storage**: No permanent retention of personal information  
- **Simulation Anonymization**: Anonymize data for simulation use
- **Regular Purging**: Clear collected data after research completion

## 🌍 **Cross-Cultural Considerations**

### **Cultural Sensitivity**
- **Name Handling**: Respectful romanization of non-Latin names
- **Cultural Context**: Consider cultural norms around public information
- **Local Laws**: Comply with local privacy and data protection laws
- **Institutional Respect**: Honor institutional and cultural privacy expectations

### **International Research Ethics**
- **GDPR Compliance**: Follow European data protection standards
- **National Regulations**: Respect national privacy laws and restrictions
- **Academic Standards**: Adhere to international academic research standards
- **Cross-border Data**: Handle international data transfers responsibly

## 🏛️ **Academic Database Ethics**

### **ORCID Guidelines**
- **Public API Only**: Use only public ORCID API endpoints
- **Researcher Consent**: Respect researcher profile privacy settings
- **Attribution**: Properly cite ORCID as data source
- **Rate Limits**: Strictly adhere to API rate limits

### **Google Scholar Ethics**
- **Public Information**: Only access publicly available profile information
- **No Automation Abuse**: Respect Google's terms of service
- **Rate Limiting**: Implement substantial delays to avoid blocking
- **Educational Use**: Use only for academic and research purposes

### **SSRN and Publication Databases**
- **Public Research Only**: Access only publicly available research papers
- **Author Attribution**: Properly attribute authors and publications
- **No Content Scraping**: Do not extract or reproduce paper content
- **Institutional Access**: Use appropriate institutional access where required

## 📊 **Research Methodology Ethics**

### **Transparency and Reproducibility**
- **Open Methods**: Document all data collection methodologies
- **Source Attribution**: Clearly identify all data sources
- **Bias Acknowledgment**: Acknowledge potential biases in data collection
- **Reproducible Process**: Provide enough detail for research reproduction

### **Accuracy and Verification**
- **Cross-Validation**: Use multiple sources to verify information
- **Uncertainty Quantification**: Clearly indicate confidence levels
- **Error Handling**: Acknowledge and handle data collection errors appropriately
- **Regular Updates**: Update methodologies as ethical standards evolve

## 🎯 **KVP Simulation Ethics**

### **Simulation-Specific Guidelines**
- **Fictional Scenarios**: Use only for hypothetical simulation scenarios
- **No Operational Targeting**: Never use for real-world operational planning
- **Educational Purpose**: Maintain focus on academic and educational objectives
- **Simulation Boundaries**: Clearly distinguish simulation from reality

### **Entity Modeling Ethics**
- **Aggregation**: Aggregate individual data into organizational patterns
- **Anonymization**: Remove or obscure personally identifying information
- **Behavioral Patterns**: Focus on organizational rather than personal behaviors
- **Uncertainty Modeling**: Include uncertainty and confidence intervals

## 🔐 **Data Security Requirements**

### **Technical Safeguards**
- **Encryption**: Encrypt all collected data during processing
- **Access Control**: Limit access to authorized researchers only
- **Secure Storage**: Use secure, temporary storage for processing
- **Network Security**: Use secure connections for all data access

### **Procedural Safeguards**
- **Access Logging**: Log all data access and processing activities
- **Regular Audits**: Conduct regular ethical compliance audits
- **Incident Response**: Maintain procedures for handling data incidents
- **Team Training**: Train all users on ethical guidelines and procedures

## 📝 **Compliance Checklist**

### **Pre-Research Checklist**
- [ ] Verify academic/educational purpose of research
- [ ] Review all target sources for public accessibility
- [ ] Confirm compliance with institutional ethics guidelines
- [ ] Set appropriate rate limits for all data sources
- [ ] Configure privacy protection measures
- [ ] Review applicable laws and regulations

### **During Research Checklist**
- [ ] Monitor for any privacy violations or sensitive data collection
- [ ] Verify all collected information is publicly available
- [ ] Maintain rate limiting and respectful access patterns
- [ ] Document all sources and attribution
- [ ] Handle any errors or issues promptly and ethically

### **Post-Research Checklist**

- [ ] Document methodology for transparency and reproducibility

## 📞 **Ethical Incident Response**

### **Reporting Procedures**
1. **Immediate Stop**: Cease all data collection activities
2. **Assessment**: Evaluate the nature and scope of the ethical issue
3. **Documentation**: Document the incident and contributing factors
4. **Notification**: Notify appropriate supervisors or ethics committees
5. **Remediation**: Take appropriate corrective actions
6. **Prevention**: Update procedures to prevent similar issues

### **Common Ethical Issues**
- **Accidental Private Data Collection**: Immediately delete and adjust selectors
- **Rate Limit Violations**: Implement longer delays and retry with courtesy
- **Terms of Service Violations**: Stop access and review compliance requirements
- **Cultural Sensitivity Issues**: Consult cultural experts and adjust approaches
