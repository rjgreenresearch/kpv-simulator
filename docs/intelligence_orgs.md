# Intelligence Organization KPV Assessments

## Purpose and Legal Basis

This module provides KPV assessments for:
1. **U.S. Federal Government national security architecture**
2. **Five Eyes partner nations** (UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand)
3. **PASS Act / adversarial nation** leadership structures

All data is derived **exclusively from publicly available, open-source
information**: government websites, think tank reports, news organisations,
academic publications, and official government communications.
No classified materials were consulted. No operational details
beyond what is publicly documented are included.

The tool is designed for:
- Academic research on organizational resilience
- National security policy analysis
- Allied coordination on key person protection
- Mirror-image analysis of adversarial KPV architecture

## The AO Component Inversion for Adversarial Organizations

For U.S. and allied organizations:
**AO = how observable the individual is to an adversary**

For adversarial organizations:
**AO = how observable the individual is to allied intelligence
         from open-source and OSINT sources**

A high AO in a Chinese organization means allied analysts can
clearly identify that role's criticality from public information.
A low AO means the role is opaque — which is itself an important
finding, as it indicates a gap in open-source coverage.

This inversion must be applied consistently when interpreting
adversarial org KPCI outputs. The `adversarial_org: true` flag
in the JSON schema signals this interpretation.

## PASS Act Context

The Protecting American Security from Kremlin Aggression Act and
related legislation, along with EO 13873 (securing the ICT supply
chain) and the NDAA foreign adversary designations, identify the
following countries as primary strategic competitors whose
organizational architecture is most relevant for KPV mirror analysis:

- People's Republic of China
- Russian Federation
- Islamic Republic of Iran
- Democratic People's Republic of Korea (North Korea)
- Republic of Cuba
- Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela (Maduro regime)

The KPVS adversarial org files cover China, Russia, Iran, and
North Korea as the primary subjects of open-source KPV analysis.

## Sources

All role and organizational information in adversarial org files
is sourced from:
- U.S. Congressional Research Service reports
- CSIS, RAND, Atlantic Council, Stimson Center analyses
- 38 North (North Korea), Carnegie Endowment, Wilson Center
- Official government directories and state media (where public)
- Academic research on adversarial organizational structures
- Stanford DigiChina, Georgetown CSET publications

Citations are embedded in the `notes` field of each role.
